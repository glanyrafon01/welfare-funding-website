# Online Consultation Survey Plan

Purpose
- Capture views of local residents about the Abercrave Welfare Hall
- Capture feedback about what work needs to be done to the Hall to make it a better community resource
- Capture community priorities for the 10-year plan.
- Gather ideas for future use of the hall.
- Build evidence for funders and elected representatives.

Format
- 6 to 8 questions total (target 5 to 7 minutes).
- Bilingual (English and Welsh) with identical question order.
- Mobile-first, short responses, plain language.
- Paper version mirrors the online questions.

Recommended question set (English)
1) Your connection to the hall (optional, checkbox)
   - Local resident
   - Young person (under 25)
   - Community group member
   - Local business
   - Elected representative
   - Potential funding partner 
   - Other (free text)

2) What do you think needs to be improved in the hall? Top priorities for the next 2 years (multi-select)
   - Heating and comfort
   - Roof and exterior protection
   - Internal insulation
   - Bar area refurbishment
   - Accessibility improvements
   - Other (free text)

3) Priorities for years 3 to 5 (multi-select)
   - Repurpose rooms to match community needs
   - Full internal redecoration
   - Install facilities for gaming, cinema or other entertainment
   - Other (free text)

4) Priorities for years 6 to 10 (multi-select)
   - Welcome new users and more diverse groups
   - Long-term financial sustainability
   - Other (free text)

5) How should the hall be used in the future? (multi-select)
   - Community groups and clubs
   - Events and performances
   - Health, wellbeing, and support services
   - Learning and skills activities
   - Youth activities
   - Social enterprise or local jobs
   - Other (free text)

6) What would make you use the hall more? (short text)

7) Any concerns or ideas we should consider? (short text)

8) Would you like updates? (optional)
   - Email or phone number
   - Consent checkbox for updates

Welsh version
- Use the same questions and order as above.
- Keep wording short and clear, avoid technical terms and jargon.

Privacy note
- Responses will be anonymised where possible and stored securely in line with GDPR.
- Contact details are optional and used only for updates.

Distribution plan
- QR codes on posters and flyers.
- Facebook posts with a short link to the survey.
- Paper forms at the hall and local venues.
- Community meeting sign-in sheets with the QR code.

Optional extras
- Add a question asking how people prefer to receive updates (email, phone, paper notice).
- Add a “postcode” question (optional) to show local reach.
